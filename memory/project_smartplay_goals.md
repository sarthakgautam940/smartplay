**Stated goal:** Turn Smartplay into a high-profit business within 1–2 years and exit via multi-million-dollar acquisition or IPO-adjacent path. UI must be visually and strategically ready for that scrutiny from day one.

**Why:** An acquirer / investor looking at the marketing site judges the brand in the first 10 seconds. Template-quality UI caps valuation no matter how good the product is.

**How to apply to every design choice:**
- Treat every page like it's in the data room of a Series A diligence. No placeholder pricing explanations, no lorem-adjacent copy, no unclaimed logos, no fake testimonials.
- Every word curated. No lies. No stats that can't be defended. When proof isn't there, use specificity of product language instead of fake social proof.
- Psychology-led leading-of-the-eye toward the primary CTA (athlete trial) — not decorative hero art that doesn't convert.
- Pricing/business-model work later will want: unit economics, pricing ladder with anchor + target + floor, outreach playbook, ICP definition, market appraisal with TAM/SAM/SOM. Hold these threads open.

**Current UI overhaul objective (this session):** Public-facing pages (home, features, pricing, about, contact, login, signup wrapper) — breathtaking visual quality, Huly/Linear/Stripe-tier composition, no backend changes, no destructive rebuilds.
