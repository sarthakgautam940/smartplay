---
name: User collaboration preferences for Smartplay
description: How the user wants to be collaborated with on Smartplay work
type: user
---

- Operates in autonomous mode; prefers action over repeated plan confirmation.
- Comfortable with long compound messages describing many issues at once — expects all of them to get addressed, not triaged down.
- Expects every design decision grounded in: graphic-design principles (hierarchy, whitespace, color, proportion), psychology (leading the eye, emotion, conversion triggers), marketing (first impression, memorability, positioning). Don't just say "I redesigned it" — say *why* each choice lands.
- Comes from a non-dev but sharp product-taste perspective: describes issues in natural language ("the text bleeds", "the cursor looks like a flashlight", "it's too dominant"). Translate those into concrete design fixes without making them feel dismissed.
- Appreciates recognition of top-tier references they name (Huly, Linear, Resend, Stripe) — meet that bar, don't downgrade.
- Long-term horizon: 1–2yr to multi-million-dollar exit. Every UI artifact should survive Series A diligence.
