# SmartPlay Public UI Overhaul Plan

## Reference Analysis

The strongest references share a few traits that separate them from template SaaS pages:

- One dominant visual idea per viewport. The best screens commit to a single cinematic scene, product canvas, or editorial composition instead of stacking equal-weight cards.
- Type behaves like a brand asset. Large, calm headlines create confidence before the user reads every word.
- Product proof appears early. Stripe-like examples use concrete workflows, metrics, and customer stories to make the claim feel operational.
- Negative space is active. Empty space guides the eye toward the CTA, then lets supporting proof breathe.
- Color is controlled. Accents are used for action and meaning, not as decorative noise.
- Motion and contrast imply progress. Light beams, field lines, and tactical marks create a sense of direction without overwhelming the content.

## Brand Persona

SmartPlay is the calm, data-literate coach beside the athlete. It is direct, prepared, encouraging, and specific. It does not shout, promise stardom, diagnose health, or pretend AI replaces people. Its visual personality is field intelligence: night-training focus, white chalk lines, live green action, sky-blue analysis, warm recovery cues, and precise soccer language.

Core emotional cues:

- Confidence without pressure.
- Progress without perfectionism.
- Data without coldness.
- Soccer seriousness without pro-club exclusivity.
- Parent trust without overreach.

## Target Audience Persona

Primary user: a 15-17 year old competitive soccer athlete balancing school, training, family logistics, and inconsistent access to elite resources. They want to improve, but they need the next action to feel possible: log the session, review the clip, adjust recovery, eat better, repeat.

Primary buyer/supporter: a parent or guardian who wants clarity without becoming the coach. They need to trust that wellness, nutrition, and progress signals are handled carefully and without pressure language.

Team decision maker: a coach or program lead who needs fast roster visibility, coachable video review, and practical development structure that works outside wealthy club ecosystems.

## Conversion Strategy

- The homepage should make SmartPlay itself the first signal, then immediately explain the product category: an athlete development OS for youth soccer.
- Primary CTA: start the 14-day athlete trial. Secondary CTA: see pricing or sign in.
- Pricing must be honest: the athlete plan is live; team and community offers are pilot/estimate paths.
- Contact should not pretend the static form sends anything. It should move real prospects to email and pilot conversations.
- Signup should feel like a guided profile build, not a generic account form.

## Page Plan

- Home: cinematic field-intelligence hero, proof rail, role paths, product system, video review story, pricing CTA.
- Features: structured product capability map across training, wellness, nutrition, mindset, video, coach feedback, and recruiting profile.
- Pricing: live athlete membership, pilot team/community paths, buyer confidence, trial logic, FAQ.
- About: mission, principles, access-aware thesis, what SmartPlay refuses to overclaim.
- Contact: pilot pathways for athletes, coaches, schools, nonprofits, and community programs with a clear email CTA.
- Login: focused access page with demo credentials and role clarity.
- Get Started: profile-building onboarding frame that sets expectations before the wizard.
